Usage Rules

Body text: Always Ink Black (#141413) on light, White on dark. Never use Signal Orange for running body text.
Signal Orange: Maximum one accent element per visible screen area. Do not use as background for large surfaces.
Contrast: Minimum 4.5:1 for body text (WCAG AA). All interactive elements must meet 3:1 against their background.
Cream backgrounds: Use Canvas Cream (#F3F0EE) for page backgrounds, Lifted Cream (#FCFBFA) for card surfaces. Never reversed.

06 — Photography

Image style

Photography establishes premium enterprise authority. Two modes: dark/technical for infrastructure, warm/human for product.

✓ Dark / Technical

High contrast, geometric, data visualization. Deep blacks with focused illumination. For hero, infrastructure sections.

✓ Human / Product

Warm, natural light. Shallow depth of field. Premium environments. Confident, not posed. For feature sections.

Flat colorful illustrations
or stock clichés

✗ Avoid

No flat illustrations, cartoons, or generic stock photography. No fake diversity-stock "smiling at laptop" shots.

07 — Voice & Tone

How we write

SX2 communicates with the precision of engineers and the authority of world-class operators. Every word earns its place.

Precise, not jargon-heavy

Use technical language where it adds clarity. Never use buzzwords to signal sophistication — the work speaks for itself.

Confident, not boastful

State facts and outcomes plainly. Let numbers and specifics carry the argument. Avoid superlatives like "best" or "leading."

Global in scope

Write for enterprise teams across markets, not a single region. Avoid idioms, colloquialisms, and culturally narrow references.

Direct with a clear ask

Every piece of copy knows its next step. CTAs are specific ("Contact Sales", "View Performance Data") — never generic ("Learn More").

08 — Don'ts

What to avoid

Common mistakes that dilute the brand's authority and distinctiveness.

🚫
No purple gradients

The brand palette is warm. No blue/purple/teal gradients. Signal orange and warm neutrals only.

🚫
No emoji in headers

Never use emoji in headlines or section headings. Reserve for very specific UI contexts (status indicators only).

🚫
No all-caps body text

Uppercase is reserved for eyebrows and labels only (12px, tracked out). Never capitalize body or heading copy.

🚫
No arbitrary spacing

Every margin, padding, and gap must map to a spacing token. No values like 17px, 23px, or 35px.

🚫
No logo modification

Do not stretch, recolor (beyond approved palette), add effects, or place the logo on busy backgrounds without a clear zone.

🚫
No generic copy

"Learn More," "Click Here," "Best-in-class," "Industry-leading." All forbidden. Every CTA must be specific and action-oriented.

🚫
No low-contrast text

Body text must meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Do not place muted-color text on cream backgrounds without checking contrast.

🚫
No mixed typefaces

Manrope only. Do not introduce a second typeface, even for code blocks or captions. Monospace font-family is the only exception.
